Ritual, with its underlying mesh of 8,000+ connected Infernet nodes, lets anyone, anywhere tap into transparent AI, with strong privacy and verifiability guardrails.

## Background

### Centralized APIs are broken

Access to AI inference is dominated by a handful of centralized operators serving models via API. These service providers geo-restrict access, require operator trust, and censor. Developer experience using these APIs is cumbersome, with limited optionality. Because of closed-source moats, these centralized model providers unilaterally price and serve compute, without any competitive market mechanism to drive cost savings.

### Infernet is a compute powerhouse

Ritual launched Infernet in November 2023 as the first decentralized oracle network for purpose-built AI workloads. Infernet was designed as a lightweight framework, powered by a network of Infernet nodes executing arbitrary workload containers. With the 1.0.0 upgrade, Ritual introduced highly requested features including on-chain payments, frameworks for verification of compute, and support for streaming responses. Today, there are 8,000+ Infernet nodes processing requests.

### Infernet shortcomings

When architecting Infernet, Ritual explicitly opted for a design that was simple yet flexible, forgoing things like coordinating nodes through consensus or integrating a robust job routing mechanism. While this enabled quickly onboarding thousands of compute providers, it inhibited adoption from a broad set of Web2 and Web3 developers.

## Bridging Infernet to Chain

### Chain-as-an-interface

The combination of the trustless properties of Ritual Chain with the expressive compute of the Infernet mesh enables truly transparent AI:
1. Resonance is extended to enable routing compute requests to Infernet nodes, providing an out-of-the-box pricing and routing mechanism for Infernet.
2. Execution sidecars are extended to tap into the broad Infernet mesh for underlying compute.
3. Flexible Web2 adapters are introduced that conform to common centralized API schemas, but abstract underlying requests silently orchestrated by Ritual Chain, with strong privacy and verifiability guardrails.
4. Web2 requests can, for the first time, be verifiable and reproducible through modular computational integrity primitives.

For Infernet node operators, this is a seamless upgrade, hyperscaling their workload demand and proportional earnings. For Web2 and Web3 users, this is a drop-in replacement to their existing workloads offering competitively priced, redundantly orchestrated, private and verifiable compute.

## Looking forward

As Ritual enables long-running precompiles, offloading asynchronous execution to the Infernet mesh will enable a lazy path to consuming computationally demanding work, while continuing to inherit the properties of the chain.
